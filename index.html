The Art of Piano Playing
for Young Pupils

For a young pianist, the first steps into the professional world are the most critical. It is not just about learning notes; it is about discovering a voice.

We believe that the time spent with a master should be a revelation. By focusing our energy into intensive, high-impact sessions, we honor the young student's natural curiosity and agility.

A Personal Connection

Every child is unique. Our 30-minute modules are intense but deeply human, allowing us to connect with the student's specific musical personality.

Philippe Raskin and Cristiana Dragos bring a shared vision to the Junior program: that excellence is achievable at any age when guided with patience, passion, and precision.

Philippe Raskin

Artistic Director & Professor

Born in Brussels, Philippe Raskin is an eclectic soloist and pedagogue whose career spans all continents. Educated at the Royal Conservatoire in Brussels under Jean-Claude Vanden Eynden, the Queen Elisabeth Music Chapel, and Madrid’s Reina Sofia under Dimitri Bashkirov, he received the Sobresaliente Prize.

A winner of the “Spanish Composers” and J.S. Bach Competitions, he has performed at the Tchaikovsky Conservatory, Musikverein, and Berlin Philharmonie. Philippe has premiered several works dedicated to him and is the Artistic Director of the César Franck International Piano Competition.

He is currently a professor at the "Friedrich Gulda" Conservatory (Vienna), the Royal Superior Institute of Music and Pedagogy (Namur) and the "Talent Music Master Courses" University of Music (Brescia).

Cristiana Dragos

Professor & Artistic Coordinator

Cristiana Dragos is a faculty member of the Cap sur la Musique Academy in Belgium. She holds both a Bachelor’s and a Master’s degree in Piano Performance from the Royal Conservatory of Brussels, where she studied under the guidance of Professor Boyan Vodenitcharov.

As a soloist, Cristiana has performed with several orchestras internationally, including in Miami, Belgium, Romania, Greece, Poland, and Croatia. She is a priweenner of over 80 international competitions throughout important musical centers such as Paris, Rome, Vienna, and Milan. Her notable distinctions include first prizes at the ‘Maria Herrero’ International Piano Competition, the ‘César Franck’ IPC, and the ‘Andrée Charlier’ Competition.

She was also awarded the Grand Prix at the Irina Satchi International Piano Competition and named an ambassador for Cluj-Napoca, Romania. Cristiana is currently undertaking postgraduate studies in Natalia Trull’s class at the Accademia del Ridotto in Italy.
